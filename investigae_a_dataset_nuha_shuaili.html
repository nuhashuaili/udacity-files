Introduction¶

Dataset Description¶

The dataset being studied is over 100,000 medical appointment records in Brazil, these statistics were taken in May 2016. The set focuses on wheather or not patients show up to appointments based on different criteria such as age, gender, medical condition, scholarship*, location of the hospital and wheather the patients were texted their appontment dates.

Questions for Analysis¶

This report will explore the follwoing questions:

  1. What is the correlation between texting patients'appointments and the number of no-shows?
  2. Is there an age group/gender discrepancy of no-shows among those who have received sms'?
  3. Is there a correlation between patients on scholarship and non attendance to appointments?
  4. What are the no show means for patients with different medical conditions?

*Scholarship is the Brazilian welfare programme also known as Bolsa Familia in Brazil

Data Cleaning¶

Based on the raw data analysis, the following changes will be made:

a) The columns that will not be used in the report will be dropped.

The dropped columns will be:
1. PatientId.
2. AppointmentID.
3. ScheduledDay.
4. AppointmentDay.
5. Neighbourhood.

b) The column names will be converted to be all lower case for ease.

c) The columns that will be renamed:

1. 'hipertension' to 'hypertension' Due to misspelling.
2. 'handcap' to 'disability' To use the correct medical term.
3. 'no-show' to 'no_show' To make the column names consistant.

d) The patient with age == -1 will be dropped as there is only one patient with that mistake so dropping will not be significant to final outcome.

e) The disability column with values more than 1 will be changed to 1, because according to the original source, this column should be either true or false.

f) The no show data type will be changed to int type, 0 for the patients who showed up and 1 for no show.

Conclusions¶

In conclusion, in this study, there seems to be a positive correlation between sending patients sms' about their appontments and no show rates, there doesn't seem to be a descrepancy in no shows in terms gender however, there are on average more younger people between the ages of 0-35 who skip thier appointments when they are texted.

Scholarship patiets has a similar no show rate that any other patient, and patients with hypertension seem to be the most likely to skip appointments.

The results above are tentative, due to the time frame being only one month, additionalyy, in comparison to the population of brazil, this sample seems to be too small. The methods of finding data in this report is limited and more advanced statistical technology needs to be used in order to get better estimates.

Further questions that can be asked are if there is a difference in attendance in different times of day, days of week or even months of year and reasonings for the discrepancy needs to be explored. Another question is patient experience and proximity to hospitals which could affect no shows. Hence, more data on patient satisfaction is needed. Finally the data given does not specify if these appointments are followups that can be switched to remote appointments or essencial appointmens that requires patients to attend.
